# MiC Policy Progress Hong Kong

## (Oct 2017) First Promotion of MiC by Hong Kong Government
* Part of the initiative to increase housing supply. This is under the previous Chief Executive, not John Lee Ka-chiu. 
* John Lee's first policy address in 2022 urged the Housing Authority to apply the MiC method to reduce waiting times for public rental housing. 
* https://builtoffsite.com.au/news/hong-kong-embraces-modular-integrated-construction-for-sustainable-urban-growth/
* https://mic.cic.hk/files/Information/55/File/1_CIC_Reference_Material_MiC_Roadmap_Report.pdf

## (Oct 2018) Promotion of MiC in Policy Address Once Again 
* Hong Kong Government actively support non-profit-making organisations to explore the feasibility of construction prefabricated modular housing
* First mention of gross floor area concessions
* https://mic.cic.hk/files/Information/55/File/1_CIC_Reference_Material_MiC_Roadmap_Report.pdf

## (2018-2019) Budget Plan 
* Finance Secretary (2018) Budget Plan earmarkeed 1 billion HKD to set up the Construction Innovation Technology Fund (CITF) for supporting the adoption of innovative construction technologies, with MiC being a priority theme. 
* Extending current gross floor area concessions for promoting green and innovative buildings to cover buildings adopting MiC
* Officially endorsed on 31 May 2019 under (PNAP) APP-161

## (2020) DEVB Policy Moves
* DEVB set out the policy on the adoption of MiC for new building works with total construction floor area (CFA) larger than 300m^2 under the Capital Works Programme to be tendered on or after 1 April 2020. 
* Likely mandated only for Government (Public) buildings 
* Policy Updated in 2024.

## (2022) JPN 8 Takes Effect
* GFA Concession increases to 10% 
* Took effect on 1 August 2022

## (April 24, 2023) The Secretary for Development visits MiC manufacturing bases in Greater Bay Area 
* During the visit, the manufacturers demonstrated to Ms Linn the modules adopted in various MiC projects and briefed her on the use of automated production lines in the factory to enhance production quality, accuracy and efficiency, and the adoption of an information management platform to coordinate the processes of procurement, manufacturing, storage and delivery of modules. 
* The manufacturers said that the overall project cost using MiC is lower than that of traditional construction methods, and that the transfer of traditional construction processes to a factory environment is also better than traditional construction methods in terms of productivity, quality management, environmental performance and occupational safety.
* The Development Bureau has established a cross-departmental steering committee to co-ordinate the development of high productivity construction methods such as MiC and streamline related approval procedures to remove barriers for the industry, with an aim to formulate measures to strengthen the supply chain and quality accreditation of MiC modules in this financial year, including the provision of manufacturing and storage sites in the Northern Metropolis, and fostering collaboration with the Greater Bay Area, with a view to expediting housing supply and strengthening the leading position of Hong Kong's construction industry in the adoption of MiC in the region. 
* https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202304/24/P2023042400615.htm

#### (11 Oct 2024) Reducing Embodied Carbon Emissions in MiC versus Traditional Conastruction [Research Paper]
* Report published (09 Oct 2024) in Nature -> Need to find, but because it focusses on carbon emissions, might not be very useful
* Embodied carbon is the CO2 created during the production, transportation and assembly of building materials. 
* MiC addresses labour shortages and speeds up construction. It also reduces waste (efficient material use) and construction time. 
* MiC resulted in a 20.7% reduction in embodied carbon emissions compared to traditional methods. 
* MiC requires less transportation of materials to the construction site, requires less heavy machinery compared to conventional construction.
* https://builtoffsite.com.au/news/reducing-embodied-carbon-emissions-in-modular-construction-versus-traditional-construction/
